My Story

I developed a keen interest in electrical engineering during my early childhood. I spent my childhood in the western part of Kazakhstan, Atyrau. This region is filled with many factories to produce electricity for its population. The factories emit a lot of greenhouse gases into the environment, making Atyrau one of the most polluted cities in Kazakhstan. I started wondering about possible alternative solutions of energy sources that reduce pollution. Solar energy was the best option due to high amount of incoming sunlight. As a result, I am inspired to apply the use of solar cells, specifically commercialization to produce eco-friendly energy source in Kazakhstan. My first step into the field of solar energy started at Nazarbayev University as a research assistant under the supervision of Prof. Askhat Jumabekov. My first two months of research assistance were dedicated to a fabrication of thin film perovskite-based solar cells in our labs. Later, I started working in modeling the devices on a computer. From that point, I started to develop interest to computer science. Specifically, I started learning and working as a front-end developer (software engineer).
